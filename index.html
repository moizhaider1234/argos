Fig. IThe Lore

A dog who simply refused to forget.

In Homer's Odyssey, Argos was a puppy when Odysseus sailed for Troy. He grew old on a dung heap, abandoned and flea-bitten, while his master fought a war and wandered the wine-dark sea for two more decades.

When Odysseus finally returned — disguised as a beggar, unrecognized by his own wife and household — it was Argos who knew him instantly. He lifted his head, wagged his tail once, and died at peace, having held on just long enough.

"He waited for Odysseus, and when he saw him, he knew." — recognition needs no disguise.

$ARGOS is built on that same patience: a community that holds through the wandering years, waiting for the return.
